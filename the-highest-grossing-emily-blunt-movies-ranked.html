The Highest-Grossing Emily Blunt Movies, Ranked

Emily Blunt is a talented British-American actress known for her excellent performances in both film and television. She has gained recognition for her roles in various genres, from drama to comedy, and has established herself as a prominent figure in the entertainment industry. Blunt's breakthrough role came with her performance in The Devil Wears Prada film, where she starred alongside Meryl Streep and Anne Hathaway. Her portrayal of the intelligent and ambitious assistant Emily earned her critical acclaim and brought her to the attention of a wider audience. Here are some of the highest-grossing movies featuring Emily Blunt, ranked based on their worldwide box office earnings.

Gnomeo & Juliet (2011) - $194.0 million

In the animated film Gnomeo & Juliet (2011), Emily Blunt lent her voice to Juliet Capulet. The film is a whimsical and humorous adaptation of William Shakespeare's famous play Romeo and Juliet, re-imagined with garden gnomes as the main characters. Juliet Capulet is one-half of the star-crossed gnome couple at the story's center. She belongs to the Blue gnome family, while her love interest, Gnomeo (voiced by James McAvoy), is from the Red gnome family. The two families have a long-standing feud, mirroring the Montague and Capulet feud in the original play.

The Huntsman: Winter's War (2016) -$164.6 million

In The Huntsman: Winter's War (2016), Emily Blunt played Freya, the Ice Queen. The film is a fantasy action adventure and a sequel to Snow White and the Huntsman (2012). Freya is the younger sister of Queen Ravenna (played by Charlize Theron) and possesses the ability to control ice and cold. After suffering heartbreak, Freya becomes embittered and desires to rule a kingdom free of love, establishing her own icy domain where love is forbidden. Emily Blunt's portrayal of Freya adds a layer of complexity to the film's fantasy world, making her a central figure in the narrative's exploration of power, family, and the human heart's capacity for light and darkness.

Into the Woods (2014) - $213.1 million

In the musical film Into the Woods (2014), Emily Blunt played the character Baker's Wife. This film is based on Stephen Sondheim and James Lapine's Broadway musical of the same name, which mixes several well-known fairy stories into a cohesive tale. The Baker's Wife is central to the film's intertwined narratives. She and her husband, the Baker (played by James Corden), are childless due to a curse placed upon their house by the Witch (played by Meryl Streep).

Jungle Cruise (2021) - $215.9 million

In Jungle Cruise (2021), Emily Blunt played the character Dr. Lily Houghton, a determined and adventurous botanist searching for a mythical healing tree in the Amazon rain forest. Emily Blunt's portrayal of Dr Lily Houghton showcases her character's intelligence, bravery, and determination. Lily's interactions with Frank Wolff combine humor, camaraderie, and chemistry, adding depth to the film's storyline. Blunt's performance brings a strong and empowered female character to the forefront of the action-packed narrative.

The Devil Wears Prada (2006) -$326.5 million

In The Devil Wears Prada (2006), Emily Blunt played the character Emily Charlton, an assistant to the formidable fashion magazine editor-in-chief Miranda Priestly, portrayed by Meryl Streep. This is a comedy-drama based on the novel of the same title by Lauren Weisberger. Emily Charlton is known for her distinctive style, sharp wit, and witty one-liners. Her performance added a layer of humor and authenticity to the film's portrayal of the fast-paced and competitive fashion world. The Devil Wears Prada became a famous and iconic film, partly due to the memorable characters like Emily Charlton that Blunt brought to life on screen.

A Quiet Place (2018) -$340.9 million

In A Quiet Place (2018), Emily Blunt played the character Evelyn Abbott, the mother of a family struggling to survive in a post-apocalyptic world overrun by blind extraterrestrial creatures with an acute sense of hearing. This horror thriller is directed by John Krasinski, who also starred alongside Blunt. Blunt's portrayal of Evelyn captures the emotional weight and tension of the character's situation. She showcases the strength and determination of a mother who will do whatever it takes to protect her children. Throughout the film, Evelyn faces physical and emotional challenges as she navigates the dangerous landscape while also dealing with the grief of past losses.

Mary Poppins Returns (2018) -$349.5 million

In Mary Poppins Returns (2018), Emily Blunt portrayed the titular character, Mary Poppins. The film is a sequel to the classic 1964 musical Mary Poppins and continues the story of the enigmatic and magical nanny who brings joy and wonder to the lives of the Banks family. Emily Blunt's portrayal of Mary Poppins was highly anticipated and widely praised for capturing the essence of the character while adding her own interpretation. In the film, Mary Poppins returns to Cherry Tree Lane in London during the Great Depression to help the grown-up Banks siblings, Michael (played by Ben Whishaw) and Jane (played by Emily Mortimer), and Michael's three children.

Edge of Tomorrow (2014) - $370.5 million

In Edge of Tomorrow (2014), Emily Blunt played Rita Vrataski, the Full Metal Bitch. Rita is a highly skilled soldier known for her exceptional combat abilities and role in humanity's fight against an alien invasion. She becomes a pivotal figure in the story due to her experience with the time loop phenomenon in which the protagonist, played by Tom Cruise, also becomes entangled. Emily Blunt's portrayal of Rita Vrataski earned her praise for portraying a strong and capable female lead in an action-packed sci-fi film.
