When a celebrity has such a devoted fanbase the way Rob does, it can be easy for people to forget they are human just like everyone else. After all, before Rob became an icon, he was just a regular guy who had a job working on a classic family show.

Of course, since Rob certainly has a unique job these days, it probably shouldn't surprise anyone that he does some pretty incredible things. A perfect example of that is Rob gets to make lots of movies with his wife Sheri Moon Zombie, including one that she didn't want to star in at first.

On top of that, there is no doubt that Rob and Sheri's relationship can be pretty unusual as evidenced by facts like they got married on Halloween. Based on Sheri and Rob's love for the horror genre, fans may assume the couple always wanted a gothic Halloween wedding. In reality, however, fans will likely be surprised to learn why Rob and Sheri tied the knot on October 31st.

Why Did Rob Zombie And Sheri Moon Zombie Get Married On Halloween?

As a musician, songwriter, record producer, scriptwriter, movie producer, and director among other things, Rob Zombie has spent his adult life expressing his inner artist. In order for Rob to be able to do that, he has to be willing to take the risk of putting himself out there to be judged by the masses.

Whether observers like any of Rob's projects or not, they have to give him credit for always being true to himself. After all, Rob's projects seem to be extremely personal to who he is as an artist and the things that have influenced him.

Given that Rob is so willing to expose his inner self, it shouldn't surprise anyone that he typically is very open during interviews. On top of that, since Sheri is a part of practically everything that Rob does, it makes sense that she seems to be equally honest and the couple have been interviewed together many times.

In 2013, The New York Post spoke to Sheri and Rob for an article that revealed a lot about the couple's relationship that many fans didn't know. A perfect example of that is the couple revealed what led to them getting married on Halloween in 2002.

At various points in Rob's career, he appeared in public in outrageous outfits while wearing makeup that drastically changed how he looked. With only that in mind, it certainly seems like Rob should absolutely adore everything about Halloween.

On top of that, Rob has made a slew of songs that embrace the gothic aesthetic and he has spent his filmmaking career mainly focused on horror films. Heck, Rob made two movies in the Halloween horror film franchise.

Based on everything that is known about Rob, it seems like a no-brainer that he would get married on Halloween. Similarly, Sheri seems to have the kind of taste that would make it very likely that she would tie the knot on October 31st.

Despite all of that, when Rob and Sheri spoke to the New York Post, he revealed that the couple didn't get married on Halloween for the reasons people assume.

“It sounds like we’re trying to be all spooky and weird, but that really wasn’t the plan.”

Following up on Rob's remarks, Sheri revealed what the couple's original plans were and how they wound up getting married on the spooky holiday.

“That was kinda by accident. We had our wedding date set for November 9. We’d been together for nine years already, and we had the wedding planner and all that stupid stuff, and we were taking a walk in the neighborhood and were like, ‘Let’s just elope.’ It was the best thing we did. I was so happy.”

Due to the assumptions that people make about Rob and Sheri, it is funny to learn that they got married on Halloween on the spur of the moment. That said, even though the couple didn't plan to get married on the spooky holiday, it seems safe to assume that they loved the idea of tying the knot on that day.
