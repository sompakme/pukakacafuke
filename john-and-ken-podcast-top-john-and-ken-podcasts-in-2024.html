The History of John and Ken Podcast

The John and Ken podcast has a rich history that spans over three decades. John Kobylt and Ken Chiampou were the original hosts of the show, which first aired on KFI AM 640. Their dynamic partnership captivated audiences with their no-nonsense approach to news and entertaining banter. Over the years, the show gained a loyal following and became one of the most listened-to podcasts in the United States.

John and Ken’s unique blend of insightful analysis and engaging conversation resonated with listeners, establishing them as trusted voices in the world of political commentary and news analysis. Their ability to tackle controversial topics and hold public figures accountable for their actions set them apart from other podcasts.

Through their informative and thought-provoking discussions, John and Ken covered a wide range of issues, including local and national politics, social issues, and current events. Listeners appreciated their honest and straightforward approach, which allowed them to gain a deeper understanding of complex topics.

As the John and Ken podcast evolved and adapted to the changing media landscape, their influence continued to grow. They became known for their fearless journalism and dedication to presenting the truth to their audience. Their in-depth interviews with newsmakers and experts provided valuable insights and shed light on important issues that often went unnoticed.

“The show has always been a platform for open and honest dialogue,” says John Kobylt, host of the John and Ken podcast. “We strive to provide our listeners with the information they need to make informed decisions and form their own opinions. It’s about challenging the status quo and holding those in power accountable.”

The John and Ken podcast’s impact extended beyond their regular episodes. They actively engaged with their audience through various social media platforms and live events, encouraging dialogue and fostering a sense of community among their fans.
