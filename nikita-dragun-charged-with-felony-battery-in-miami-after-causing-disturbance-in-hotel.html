Nikita Dragun Charged With Felony Battery In Miami After Causing Disturbance In Hotel

Publish date: 2024-08-24

YouTuber, make-up artist, and model Nikita Dragun was handcuffed as she exited a posh Miami Beach hotel after police said she doused security personnel and officers with water while wandering about the pool in her underwear.

It appears that the transgender influencer was having too much fun at The Goodtime Hotel when she was arrested Monday night on a felony allegation of violence on a police officer.

The YouTuber — whose real name is Nikita Nguyen — had allegedly been walking around the pool area in her bathing suit and being disorderly for hours, per TMZ.

Hotel staff claimed that when they asked her to put her clothes back on, she intentionally threw water on them, which is when the cops were called.

Police claim that when they arrived, hotel security informed them that Nikita had been causing a commotion and roaming the pool area naked for a considerable amount of time.

Police said security reportedly told them Nikita poured water on hotel employees on purpose and disobeyed orders to stop causing the disturbance, according to Marca.

Officers said that as soon as they arrived on the incident, security led them up to Nikita's room where they heard loud music being played.

After many knocks, according to the police, Nikita allegedly opened the door. However, when hotel security told her to stop talking to them or they would ask her to leave, Nikita allegedly slammed the door in their faces.

"Do you want more?" Nikita allegedly asked security a few seconds later when she reopened the door, according to police. Nikita allegedly threw an open water bottle, which caused water to splash and spill on both the security guard and the officer, leading to her arrest, according to the police.

Nikita was arrested for misdemeanor battery, misdemeanor disorderly conduct, and misdemeanor battery on a police officer. She was taken into custody at the Turner Guilford Knight Correctional Center in Miami-Dade County with a $5,000 bond.
