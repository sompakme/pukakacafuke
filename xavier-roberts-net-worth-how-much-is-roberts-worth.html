Xavier Roberts, born in Cleveland, Georgia in October 1955, had a passion for creating dolls from a young age. In the late 1970s, Roberts introduced his first line of dolls called Little People, which garnered attention and popularity.

Encouraged by the success of his dolls, Roberts founded Original Appalachian Artworks, Inc. This marked the start of his official career in the toy manufacturing industry. Setting up his production facility at Babyland General Hospital, a converted medical clinic, Roberts embarked on a journey to bring his unique doll creations to a wider audience.

It was in 1982 that Xavier Roberts made a breakthrough that would shape his career and solidify his place in the toy industry.
